title: Dal Paratha from the left over Dal
---

Traditionally Dal Parathas are cooked with stuffed spicy dry dal mixture. But this recipe is different, I cooked the Dal Paratha with the leftover dal from the previous night dinner. Weekend or especially Saturday's breakfast is heavy keeping in mind to catch up with family. But I always use this method when my dal is little thick and spicy to give that extra flavor to my paratha.

Add some spices that just adds more flavors, I add some ajwain (the basic of paratha) to improve the digestion. The coriander leaves for the flavor and some teeny tiny bits of onion to add that crunch. I serve them hot with homemade curd and some pickle and a cup of tea for my morning breakfast.

Ingredients:

1. Whole Wheat Flour - 2 cups
2. Leftover Dal Tadka - 1 cup
3. Onion - 1/4 cup, chopped finely
4. Green chilies - 2, finely chopped
5. Coriander Leaver - 1 cup, finely chopped
6. Ajwain - 2 tsp
7. Red Chili Powder - 1 tsp
8. Coriander Powder - 2 tsp
9. Cumin Powder - 1 tsp
10. Salt - according to taste
11. Turmeric Powder - a small pinch
12. Clarified Butter or Ghee - to cook the parathas

Steps of Preparation:

1. Knead the Dal and Wheat Flour, along with all the spices together to make a soft and smooth dough.
2. Pinch a small portion of the dough and make equal sized balls.
3. Roll these balls to relatively thin round disc-shaped parathas
4. To make the Parathas, heat a griddle.
5. Once the griddle is heated, cook the Parathas until its golden brown on both sides.
6. Serve the Parathas with the choice of your accompaniments.
