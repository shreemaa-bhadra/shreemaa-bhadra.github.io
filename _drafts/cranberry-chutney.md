Last week I ordered a pack of Cranberries, seeing its abundance at this time of the year. The moment I tasted Cranberries for the first time, it made me go back to the Spring of Odisha where we have an abundance of Bara Koli (Indian Jujube). Being a kid I loved the Bara Koli, every time my grandma picked some for making pickles, that finished on the way of Pickling. So when I found cranberries so much similar to the taste, I kind of made my mind to make the Odia way we cook our meetha khatta (sweet chutney), it's the basic temper with curry leaves and panch phoran and sweeten with jaggery.

You can serve with rice platter we eat, or spread on your chapati and enjoy a sweet and sour simple roll.
