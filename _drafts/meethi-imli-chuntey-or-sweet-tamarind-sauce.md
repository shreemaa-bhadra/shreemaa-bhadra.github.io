title: Meethi Imli Chuntey or Sweet Tamarind Sauce
---

If you are an Indian Chaat Lover and haven't encountered this sweet and sour sauce, then it's a big lie. Every Indian Chaat is garnished with this Chutney, it's basically made with tamarind spiced up with minimal spices to flavor it, sweetened with dates and jaggery or sugar. And sometimes this chutney, I spread over my hot Chapatis or Rotis and pouring generously and spreading across each corner and rolling them for my sometime breakfast. The sweet and sour flavor do the trick of flavoring anything and everything in this food world.

So here goes the basic khatti meethi Imli ki Chutney (sweet and sour tamarind sauce):

Ingredients:

1. Tamarind - 1 cup, tightly packed
2. Dates - 1 cup (i have used pitted ones and soaked it overnight to make it tender)
3. Roasted Cumin and red chili powder - 1 tsp
4. Dry Ginger or saunth - 1 tsp
5. Hing or Asofoetida - 1 pinch
6. Vegetable oil or any unflavored oil - 1 tsp
7. Water - according to the desired consistency
8. Salt - 1 pinch
9. Sugar  or powdered jaggery- 1/2 cup

Steps of Preparation:

1. Soak the Tamarind and dates for 3-4 hours in the desired quantity of water.
2. Heat a frying pan, add oil to it.
3. In the mean time, squeeze out the tamarind and get the smooth pulp out of it.
4. Grind the dates to bring a smooth texture.
5. To the hot oil, add the asofoetida and add the tamarind pulp.
6. Then to the pulp, add the dry ginger powder and roasted cumin and chili powder and let it simmer for 5-6 mins.
7. After that add the sugar or jaggery cook and melts and mixes with the mixture.
8. Once the tamarind is cooked and the raw smell goes off, add water for the required consistency.
