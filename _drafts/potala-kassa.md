Come April and this veggie becomes staple in my home. It's one of the best veggie I have encountered and is well accepted in my family. I rarely get bored of this veggie, reason being it's texture. There's this crunch which makes me crave for it for the rest of the year. My family back home is true believer of eating local and seasonal. So we just stick to this veggie for the summer months when it's grown in Odisha and is available in plenty. But here in States I don't find them in true size and shape most of time. But sometimes the nostalgic me, recreates the memory in every summer this wonderful Potala Kassa the Odia way.

So the dish Potala Kassa is a dry spicy dish, usually served with Rice and Dal or Chapati. It's basically cooked in whole spices and caramelized onion with the basic ginger garlic paste to flavor and give the dish the proper spice. The dish is good main course and is almost a summer staple in many Odia homes.
