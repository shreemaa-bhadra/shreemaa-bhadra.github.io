Chutneys are the greatest accompaniment with all the Indian dishes. They are typically served with the main course to increase the nutritional content of the meal. South Indian dishes like Idli, Dosas, and Upma go well with this coconut chutney. Its creamy, mildly sweet flavor gives the food a peculiar mouthfeel. It's a family favorite, and because only a few ingredients are needed, even my boys can quickly prepare it.

## Ingredients for Coconut Chutney:

1. Roasted Channa  Dal (Bhaja Buta Dali) - 3tbsp
2. Grated Coconut / shredded coconut (dried/frozen/fresh) - 1 cup
3. Cumin / Jeera - 1tsp
4. Ginger -  1 inch
5. Salt
6. Green Chili - 2
7. Small ball of Tamarind

## Ingredients for Tempering:

1. Oil of choice, I use sesame oil.
2. Hing (asofoetida) - 1 pinch
3. Mustard Seeds. - 1 tsp
4. Curry Leaves - 1 Sprig

## Steps of Preparation for Coconut Chutney:

1. Take roasted channa dal, coconut, green chillies, ginger cube, cumin and salt and grind to a paste, add water in small quantity in batches to bring down to the desired consistency.
2. Take a small pan, add Oil and once its heated add mustard seeds, whole red chillies and curry leaves.

Eat it with dosa or idly.
