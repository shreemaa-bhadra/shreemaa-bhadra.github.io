Lemonade is the first-ever recipe I learned. Oh no, it's not at all fun to hone the simple recipes. It does take an effort to reach to be perfect in the [basics](https://cookspan.com/tag/basics/). My faint memory says I used to proudly flaunt this skill of mine in my childhood days, serving my family and friends. Nothing can beat the freshness of the lemonade, it's refreshing, it's soothing and it's so so light. And more to it, it creates family memories worth so many generations.

With everything so easily available these days in store, grabbing a lemonade from the store to your glass is so easy. But it takes away so much fun. This whole thought made me document this very simple recipe. I added some crushed sprigs of homegrown chocolate mint to give them some extra edge.

![Lemonade](/assets/uploads/2020/06/DSC_9412-scaled.jpg) Lemonade

### Ingredients for Lemonade:

1. Lemon Juice - 2 tsp
2. Sugar - 2 tbsp
3. Water - 1 large glass
4. Chocolate Mint - 6-7 leaves chopped and crushed

### Steps of Preparation:

1. The trick is to bring and mix the sugar with the normal temperature of the water.
2. Then add the mint and the lemon juice.
3. Finally, add some ice cubes or some splash of cold water.

Serve every summer this refreshing drink to your friends and family and recreate the summer fun.
