Indian Street Food has been a never ending story. Every part of India has its flavor in its streets. And we Indians are crazy about it no matter what. But the monsoon season is a threat when you feel hygiene is the biggest concern. But why not try them out at home and gulp the crazy cravings in a weekend evening or night.

The thoughts, I scribbled above, were all mine today, when me and my husband wanted a break from the normal Chapati-Subzi dinner. The very idea of making veg-roll at home came to me.

It's easy and a treat to your taste buds and for a change you feel wonderful when the '_Thela'_  (movable food stalls) is all set at your home with 100% hygienic and the oil factor is controlled by you too.

The recipe of Veg  Roll goes like this:

**Ingredients:**

_For the Filling:_

[![Veggies](/assets/uploads/2015/07/Veggies.jpg)](/assets/uploads/2015/07/Veggies.jpg) Veggies

1. Cabbage - 1/4 medium sized, finely chopped
2. Carrot - 1 finely grated
3. Onions - 1 finely sliced
4. Vegetable Oil - 1 tbsp
5. Pepper - 2 tsp
6. Salt - According to the taste

_For the Chapati or the covering:_

1. All purpose Flour or Maida - 1 cup
2. Vegetable Oil - 1tsp
3. Salt according to the taste

_For Assembling:_

1. Tissue Paper
2. Onion - 1 medium sized, finely sliced
3. Chili Sauce - 2 tbsp
4. Tomato Ketchup - 4 tbsp

**Steps of Preparation:**

_For the Chapati or Covering:_

1. Take 1 cup of  all purpose flour (Maida) add 1 tsp salt and 1 tsp oil to it.
    
    [![Cover - Step 1](/assets/uploads/2015/07/Cover-Step-1.jpg)](/assets/uploads/2015/07/Cover-Step-1.jpg) Cover - Step 1
2. Add water slowly to the mixture and knead the flour.
3. Knead it till you make a soft dough out of it.
    
    [![Cover - Step 3](/assets/uploads/2015/07/Cover-Step-3.jpg)](/assets/uploads/2015/07/Cover-Step-3.jpg) Cover - Step 3
4. After the dough is done, keep it for 1 hour.
5. Make small balls out of it and roll it into round shape.
    
    [![Cover - Step 5](/assets/uploads/2015/07/Cover-Step-5.jpg)](/assets/uploads/2015/07/Cover-Step-5.jpg) Cover - Step 5
    
    [![Cover - Step 6](/assets/uploads/2015/07/Cover-Step-6.jpg)](/assets/uploads/2015/07/Cover-Step-6.jpg) Cover - Step 6
6. Heat the tawa or griddle.
7. Add the rolled dough into the dough. Let it cook for 1-2 mins till the bubbles come out.
    
    [![Cover - Step 7](/assets/uploads/2015/07/Cover-Step-7.jpg)](/assets/uploads/2015/07/Cover-Step-7.jpg) Cover - Step 7
8. Turn and let it cook till the bubbles get browned.
9. Add the oil to it and let the covering get a golden brown color.
    
    [![Cover - Step 8](/assets/uploads/2015/07/Cover-Step-8.jpg)](/assets/uploads/2015/07/Cover-Step-8.jpg) Cover - Step 8

_For the filling:_

1. Heat a pan. Once the pan is heated, add oil to it.
2. Add the sliced onions and saute it for 1-2 mins.
    
    [![Filling - Step 2](/assets/uploads/2015/07/Filling-Step-2.jpg)](/assets/uploads/2015/07/Filling-Step-2.jpg) Filling - Step 2
3. Once the onions get translucent, add the finely chopped cabbage and cook it till the water is released from the cabbage and it gets dried up.
    
    [![Filling - Step 3](/assets/uploads/2015/07/Filling-Step-3.jpg)](/assets/uploads/2015/07/Filling-Step-3.jpg) Filling - Step 3
4. Add the grated carrot and cook it for 1-2 mins.
    
    [![Filling - Step 4](/assets/uploads/2015/07/Filling-Step-4.jpg)](/assets/uploads/2015/07/Filling-Step-4.jpg) Filling - Step 4
    
    [![Filling - Step 51](/assets/uploads/2015/07/Filling-Step-51.jpg)](/assets/uploads/2015/07/Filling-Step-51.jpg) Filling - Step 5
5. Then finally add salt according to taste and 2 tsp pepper powder.
    
    [![Filling - Step 61](/assets/uploads/2015/07/Filling-Step-61.jpg)](/assets/uploads/2015/07/Filling-Step-61.jpg) Filling - Step 5.1
    
    [![Filling - Step 62](/assets/uploads/2015/07/Filling-Step-62.jpg)](/assets/uploads/2015/07/Filling-Step-62.jpg) Filling - Step 6

_For Assembling:_

[![Assembling - Ingredients](/assets/uploads/2015/07/Assembling-Ingredients.jpg)](/assets/uploads/2015/07/Assembling-Ingredients.jpg) Assembling - Ingredients

1. Take the tissue paper and fold it into half.
    
    [![Assemble Step -1](/assets/uploads/2015/07/Assemble-1.jpg)](/assets/uploads/2015/07/Assemble-1.jpg) Assemble Step -1
2. Add the Chapati or Roll covers in such a way that half of it stays on the tissue paper.
    
    [![Assemble -2](/assets/uploads/2015/07/Assemble-2.jpg)](/assets/uploads/2015/07/Assemble-2.jpg) Assemble -2
3. Brush 1 tbsp of tomato ketchup and 1/2 tbsp of chili sauce on the surface of the Roll cover.
    
    [![Assemble - 3](/assets/uploads/2015/07/Assemble-3.jpg)](/assets/uploads/2015/07/Assemble-3.jpg) Assemble - 3
    
    [![Assemble - 4](/assets/uploads/2015/07/Assemble-4.jpg)](/assets/uploads/2015/07/Assemble-4.jpg) Assemble - 4
4. Place the sliced onions in the center of the cover in a vertical diameter.
    
    [![Assemble - 5](/assets/uploads/2015/07/Assemble-5.jpg)](/assets/uploads/2015/07/Assemble-5.jpg) Assemble - 5
5. Add the cooked filling above the sliced onions.
    
    [![Assemble - 6](/assets/uploads/2015/07/Assemble-6.jpg)](/assets/uploads/2015/07/Assemble-6.jpg) Assemble - 6
6. Roll the paper and the cover together.
