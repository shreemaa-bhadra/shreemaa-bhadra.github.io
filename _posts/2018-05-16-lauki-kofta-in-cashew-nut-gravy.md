Lauki or Bottle Gourd is a very healthy vegetable with 96 % water content. This is a must for the summers. I love this veggie in any format. The Lauki Channa Dal was a family favorite until I made this creamy North Indian Delight of Lauki Kofta cooked in onion, tomatoes and cashew gravy. I had heard of this dish from all my North Indian friends. Though Lauki was a regular veggie in my family I never cooked it this way. Last weekend I was left with half of Lauki and no other veggie. So to fulfill the weekend special demand of both the boys I made Lauki Kofta in Cashew nut Gravy recipe with ghee smeared Rotis and some cucumber Raita.

![Lauki Kofta in Cashew Gravy](/assets/uploads/2018/05/DSC_5660.jpg) Lauki Kofta in Cashew Gravy

Lauki ka Kofta is a flavorful, creamy gravy dish which you can serve for your parties and have it on special occasions.

Here goes the recipe of Lauki Kofta in Creamy Cashew nut Gravy:

### Ingredients for Lauki Kofta in Cashew nut Gravy:

#### For the Kofta:

1. Bottle Gourd - 1 and 1/2 cups, grated
2. Gram Flour or Besan - 4 tbsp
3. Red Chili Powder -1 tsp
4. Coriander Powder - 1 tsp
5. Cumin Powder - 1 tsp
6. Turmeric Powder - 1 pinch
7. Salt - according to taste
8. Canola Oil - for deep frying

#### For the Gravy:

1. Onions - 3/4 cup, finely chopped
2. Tomatoes - 1 cup, cubed or chopped
3. Ginger Garlic Paste - 1 tsp
4. Oil - 2 tbsp
5. Cumin seeds - 1 tsp
6. Cashew nuts - 1/4 cup
7. Red Chili Powder - 1 tsp
8. Coriander Powder - 1 tsp
9. Cumin Powder - 1 tsp
10. Kasuri Methi - 1 tsp
11. Turmeric  Powder - 1 tsp
12. Salt - according to taste
13. Sugar - 1/2 tsp

### Steps of Preparation for the Lauki Kofta in Cashew nut Gravy:

#### Preparing Koftas:

1. Grate the Lauki or Bottle Gourd finely. Squeeze out excess water and reserve the water for using it later for the gravy.
    
    ![Grated Lauki or Bottle Gourd](/assets/uploads/2018/05/DSC_5562.jpg) Grated Lauki or Bottle Gourd
    
    ![Squeezing the excess water](/assets/uploads/2018/05/DSC_5567.jpg) Squeezing the excess water
2. To the Lauki, add the gram flour and all the dry powdered masalas (turmeric powder, cumin powder, coriander powder, salt) and mix uniformly.
    
    ![Lauki with Gram Flour and Powdered Spices](/assets/uploads/2018/05/DSC_5574.jpg) Lauki with Gram Flour and Powdered Spices
3. Gently form round balls and keep aside.
4. Take a Kadhai or wok and heat the oil, then fry the balls until golden brown.
    
    ![Add the Lauki balls for frying](/assets/uploads/2018/05/DSC_5585.jpg) Add the Lauki balls for frying
    
    ![Fry till golden brown](/assets/uploads/2018/05/DSC_5588.jpg) Fry till golden brown
5. Strain out the excess oil and keep aside the Koftas to be dunked into the gravy.
    
    ![Fried Koftas](/assets/uploads/2018/05/DSC_5595.jpg) Fried Koftas

#### Preparing the Gravy:

1. Heat a pan with oil (I used canola oil).
2. Add the cumin seeds to the oil and let it splutter.
    
    ![Cumin Tempering](/assets/uploads/2018/05/DSC_5599.jpg) Cumin Tempering
3. Then add the chopped onions and fry till softened.
    
    ![Adding the Onions](/assets/uploads/2018/05/DSC_5602.jpg) Adding the Onions
4. Add the Ginger Garlic paste and cook till the raw smell goes off.
    
    ![Ginger Garlic Paste added](/assets/uploads/2018/05/DSC_5605.jpg) Ginger Garlic Paste added
5. Tomatoes are further added and cooked till the water is dried up.
    
    ![Adding Tomatoes](/assets/uploads/2018/05/DSC_5608.jpg) Adding Tomatoes
6. Then add the cashew nuts and saute for a minute and let it cool.
    
    ![Adding Cashewnuts](/assets/uploads/2018/05/DSC_5610.jpg) Adding Cashew nuts
    
    ![Cooked Masalas for the gravy](/assets/uploads/2018/05/DSC_5621.jpg) Cooked Masalas for the gravy
7. Make a fine paste with the cooked onions, tomatoes and cashews.
    
    ![Making a paste of cooked onions, tomatoes and cashews](/assets/uploads/2018/05/DSC_5624.jpg) Making a paste of cooked onions, tomatoes and cashews
8. Heat the same pan, add a teaspoon of oil and the ground paste and then add the powdered spices (cumin powder, coriander powder, red chili powder, turmeric powder and salt).
    
    ![Adding a tsp of oil](/assets/uploads/2018/05/DSC_5626.jpg) Adding a tsp of oil
    
    ![](/assets/uploads/2018/05/DSC_5627.jpg)
    
    ![Adding powdered spices to the gravy](/assets/uploads/2018/05/DSC_5637.jpg) Adding powdered spices to the gravy
9. Saute and cook the gravy. Add the squeezed out water from bottle gourd, you can add an additional amount of water based on the gravy consistency you are looking for. Let it simmer for a minute or two.
10. Crush the Kasuri Methi and add to the simmering gravy to add more flavors.
     
     ![Adding Kasuri Methi](/assets/uploads/2018/05/DSC_5642.jpg) Adding Kasuri Methi
11. Add the Koftas to the gravy and serve or you can directly add the koftas while serving.
