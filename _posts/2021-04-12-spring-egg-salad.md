Spring is the season of new beginnings. There are sprouting greens, a pleasant breeze, bright flowers, and a promise of lot of happiness. It is  nature's way of saying that we've made it through the harsh winters and are on our way to happier times. I also enjoy spring because of the abundance of fresh produce; there are tender greens all around that are ideal for a plateful of salad. I always try to make a sumptuous salad bowl out of whatever I have in the pantry. I use different textures and a light vinaigrette dressing. Because it was Easter last weekend and there were [eggs](https://cookspan.com/category/non-vegeterian/egg/) everywhere, I decided to keep my Spring Egg Salad bowl in the theme, adding so much spring to my platter. It's colorful, bright, and vibrant, with a sweet and citrusy flavor.

![Spring Egg Salad](/assets/uploads/2021/04/IMG_2214-scaled.jpeg)

Here goes my detailed recipe for the Spring Egg Salad:

### Ingredients for the Salad:

1. Romaine Lettuce : 1 head
2. Avocado : 1
3. Red Onion - 1/4 cup , sliced
4. Blue berries :  1/2 cup
5. Cherry Tomates : 1/2 cup
6. Boiled Eggs : 4
7. Sunflower Seeds : 1/4 cup, roasted
8. English Cucumber :  1![Spring Egg Salad Ingredients](/assets/uploads/2021/04/IMG_2212-scaled.jpeg)

### Ingredients for Dressing :

1. Olive Oil : 1/4 cup
2. Lemon Juice : 1/4 cup
3. Garlic Powder : 2 tsp
4. Onion Powder : 2 tsp
5. Pepper : 1 tsp
6. Paprika : 1 tsp
7. Cumin Powder : 1tsp
8. Salt - according to taste

### Steps of Preparation for Spring Egg Salad:

1. Wash the vegetables thoroughly and roughly chop them into bite-sized pieces.
2. After chopping the romaine lettuce, toss it with half of the dressing and season generously.
3. Then add the remaining vegetables and toss well.
4. Season the salad with the remaining dressing. Chop and add the eggs to the well-mixed and seasoned vegetables.
5. For an extra crunch, sprinkle with pumpkin seeds or pepitas.

![](/assets/uploads/2021/04/IMG_1760-scaled.jpeg)

You can enjoy the salad as it is or stuff in a tortilla to make a wrap or stuff in between the bread for a sandwich.
